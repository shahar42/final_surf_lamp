Our Commitment to Accessibility

Surf Lamp is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We are continually improving the user experience for everyone and applying the relevant accessibility standards.

Conformance Standards

Surf Lamp strives to conform to the Israeli Standard (IS) 5568 and Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.0 at the AA level. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible to people with disabilities and user-friendly for everyone.

Current Accessibility Features

  • Semantic HTML structure for screen reader compatibility
  • Keyboard navigation support for all interactive elements
  • High contrast color scheme meeting WCAG contrast ratio requirements
  • Responsive design that works across devices and screen sizes
  • Clear visual hierarchy and consistent navigation
  • Alternative text descriptions for meaningful images and icons
  • Form labels and error messages for accessible form interaction

Known Limitations

While we work to maintain the highest accessibility standards, some areas of our service may have limitations:

  • LED visualization graphics may not be fully accessible to screen readers (alternative text descriptions provided)
  • Real-time data updates may not always announce to assistive technologies

We are actively working to address these limitations in future updates.

Technical Specifications

Surf Lamp relies on the following technologies to work with your web browser and assistive technologies:

  • HTML5
  • CSS3
  • JavaScript
  • ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications) attributes

Assessment and Testing

Surf Lamp's accessibility has been assessed through:

  • Self-evaluation and internal testing
  • Keyboard navigation testing
  • Color contrast analysis tools
  • Screen reader compatibility testing

Continuous Improvement

This accessibility statement will be reviewed and updated as we continue to improve the accessibility of Surf Lamp. We are dedicated to making our web application accessible to the widest possible audience.

This statement was created in accordance with the requirements of the Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Act, 1998 and Israeli Standard IS 5568.
